 Lesson Plan: Integrating Drama, Mime, or Theater Techniques into a History Class on the Opium Wars

Day 1:

1. Introduction (10 minutes)
	* Briefly explain the Opium Wars to the students, focusing on the key events, causes, and consequences.
	* Discuss the importance of understanding history and how drama can help students connect with the past.
2. Warm-up (15 minutes)
	* Have students participate in a series of drama exercises to loosened up and get comfortable with expressing themselves creatively.
	* Exercises can include improvisation, role-playing, or movement activities related to the Opium Wars.
3. Scriptwriting (15 minutes)
	* Divide students into small groups.
	* Provide each group with a specific event or character from the Opium Wars (e.g., a soldier, a merchant, a politician, etc.).
	* Guide students in writing a short script (5-10 minutes) that showcases the perspectives and experiences of their assigned character.
4. Rehearsal (15 minutes)
	* Give students time to rehearse their scripts in their groups.
	* Encourage them to use movement, facial expressions, and dialogue to bring their characters to life.
5. Performance (15 minutes)
	* Have each group present their script to the class.
	* After each performance, facilitate a brief discussion about the themes and messages presented.
6. Reflection (5 minutes)
	* Ask students to reflect on the experiences of the characters they portrayed and how it relates to the broader context of the Opium Wars.
7. Homework (due before the next class)
	* Have students write a brief reflection paper discussing the role of drama in understanding history and how their performance contributed to their understanding of the Opium Wars.
