The central dogma of molecular biology is a fundamental principle of molecular biology that states that information flows in a specific manner within cells. Specifically, it states that DNA information is transcribed into RNA, which is then translated into protein. This process is crucial for cellular function and is central to all biological processes.

The central dogma was proposed by Francis Crick and James Watson in their landmark 1953 paper on the structure of DNA. They based their hypothesis on their understanding of DNA replication, transcription, and translation, which they had discovered through their research on the structure of DNA.
