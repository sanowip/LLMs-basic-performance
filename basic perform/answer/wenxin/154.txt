Lesson Plan: Opium Wars between China and Britain

1. To understand the historical background of the Opium Wars.
2. To analyze the impact of foreign policies on local communities.

3. Timer for timing each activity.
4. Chalkboard for taking notes and discussion.

1. Introduce the topic of the Opium Wars and discuss its importance in world history (5 minutes).

3. Acting Warm-up: Introduce basic acting techniques such as facial expressions, body language, and dialogue (5 minutes).
4. Drama Scene 1: Role-playing - British Officers and Chinese Citizens (15 minutes)
	* Students will role-play interactions between British officers and Chinese citizens during the Opium Wars.
	* Through acting, students will learn about cultural differences and conflicts between the two sides.

	* Using simple props (such as sticks and stones), students will create mime scenes depicting military strategies used during the Opium Wars.
	* Through this activity, students will gain an understanding of military tactics and strategies.
6. Closing and Homework: Review key points from today's lesson and ask students to prepare for Day 2's lesson (5 minutes).

Day 2: Historical Analysis and Group Discussions

1. Introduce today's topic - the impact of foreign policies on local communities (5 minutes).
2. Drama Scene 2: Opium Trade and its Impact on Chinese Citizens (15 minutes)
	* Students will role-play interactions between merchants, officials, and citizens regarding the opium trade during the Opium Wars.
	* Through acting, students will gain a deeper understanding of the impact of opium trade on Chinese society.
3. Mime Scene 2: Reenactment of the Opium War Battlefield (10 minutes)
	* Using simple props, students will create a scene depicting a battlefield during the Opium War.
	* Through this activity, students will gain an understanding of military tactics and historical geography.
4. Group Discussions: Students will divide into groups and discuss how foreign policies affected local communities during the Opium Wars (20 minutes).
	* Students will use their research from Day 1 to support their discussion.
5. Closing and Homework: Review key points from today's lesson and prepare for tomorrow's final exam (5 minutes).

Day 3: Final Exam and Closing Remarks

2. Final Exam: Students will answer questions related to the Opium Wars, focusing on historical background, impact of foreign policies, and local communities (20 minutes).
3. Drama Scene 3: Reenactment of Peace Treaty Signing (5 minutes)
	* Through acting, students will recreate an iconic scene from the Opium Wars - the signing of the peace treaty between China and Britain.

5. Closing Remarks: Thank students for their participation and encourage them to continue learning about history (5 minutes).
